About

Hey — I’m James Raines! I’m a geospatial researcher, programmer, and analyst. I have been a USGS grant recipient to study land cover change, developed scripts to aggregate and automate large data sets for the City and County of Denver, and used GIS to help expand services for food banks.

I like to build web maps, work on conservation issues and with non-profits, and am a huge open-source advocate. I have written on subjects including natural protected areas, monarch butterflies, and web and mobile cartography. However, I am especially interested in geospatial data science.
